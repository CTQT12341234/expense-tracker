❓ Help & Glossary
📊 Dashboard▸

Income / Expenses / Saved / Net — your four headline numbers for the current month. Net = Income − Expenses − Savings deposited.

Allocation tracker — compares how much you planned to spend/save per bucket (Needs, Wants, etc.) against what you've actually spent so far this month. Tap any bucket to jump to those transactions.

Spending by category donut — breaks down your month's expenses by category (Food, Transport, etc.).

Trend chart — compares your income vs expenses over the selected period (Week / 1M / 6M / 1Y / 5Y / YTD). Useful for spotting patterns.

Recent transactions — the 5 most recent entries. Respects your current View (All / Me / Partner).

View switcher — top bar with All / Me / Partner / Shared. Filters the entire dashboard to show only that person's numbers.

📋 Transactions▸

Bucket — a category for how money is allocated: Needs (rent, food, bills), Wants (entertainment, dining out), Investment (stocks, ETFs), Savings (deposits to goals). Auto-detected from the category or description, or set manually.

Bucket auto-rules — keyword rules in Settings that automatically assign a bucket. E.g. "netflix" → Wants.

Member badge — coloured pill showing who the transaction belongs to (Me, Partner, Shared). Set in the Edit modal.

How shared transactions work

There are two kinds of expenses:

Personal — belongs 100% to one person. Tagged to Me or Partner.

Shared — belongs to both people. Stored as full amount, split into each person's portion.

Example — Groceries $100, split 50/50:

• View All → $100 (full amount, counted once — no double-counting)
• View Me → $50 (my share only)
• View Partner → $50 (their share only)
• View Shared → $100 (full amount with split breakdown: Me $50 · Partner $50)

Split types:
⚖️ 50/50 — each person pays half
✏️ Custom — you set the % (e.g. 70/30)
👤 No split — appears in Shared and All views only

Formula:
All = personal(Tin) + personal(Partner) + shared(full)
Me = personal(Me) + shared(my portion)
Partner = personal(Partner) + shared(their portion)

½ split badge — shown on transactions in your individual view to indicate the amount is your share of a larger shared expense.

Recurring badge 🔁 — marks transactions that match a recurring rule. They are auto-posted from the Recurring tab.

🏦 Savings▸

Income allocation plan — divide your income into buckets by percentage. E.g. 50% Needs, 20% Wants, 5% Investment, 25% Savings. The percentages must add to 100%.

This month's allocation — enter your income and it calculates the dollar amount for each bucket. Tap "+ Record $X" to pre-fill a savings deposit or investment entry.

Savings goals — named targets (e.g. Emergency Fund $5,000). Deposits are linked to a goal to track progress. Tap any goal to edit it.

Record a savings movement — manually log a deposit or withdrawal. Link it to a goal and a member.

Investment log — records of assets you've bought or sold (ETFs, crypto, property). Tagged to a member so each person's investments are separate.

📈 Investment tracker — tracks how much you're investing this quarter vs your allocation target.

Quarterly income — total income received across all 3 months of the current quarter (Jan–Mar for Q1, Apr–Jun for Q2, etc.).

Target — your investment allocation % × quarterly income. If you earn $9,000 in Q1 and your Investment bucket is 5%, your target is $450.

Invested so far — total of all investment records logged this quarter. Turns amber at 80%, red when over target.

Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4 Progress % — invested so far ÷ target × 100. E.g. 74% means you've invested $333 of a $450 quarterly target. Over 100% means you exceeded the target.

What if I'm in Q2? — the tracker automatically shows the current quarter. In Q2 (Apr–Jun) it shows April–June income and investments. Q1 data is still visible in the investment log history.

Rebalance suggestion — appears when you've been consistently over your investment % for 2+ of the last 3 months. It suggests bumping your Investment allocation % up to match your actual behaviour, and reducing another bucket (usually Wants) to keep the total at 100%. Tap "✅ Apply suggestion" to update automatically.

On track — means your 3-month average investment % is within range of your target. No action needed.

🔁 Recurring▸

Recurring transactions — bills or income that repeat on a schedule (weekly, monthly, yearly). E.g. rent, Netflix, salary.

Est. monthly net — the banner at the top shows your estimated monthly net cashflow from all recurring rules combined. Positive means recurring income exceeds recurring expenses.

Post to this month — manually adds all recurring transactions to your transaction list for the current month. Only adds ones not already posted (checks name + category + month).

Frequency — Weekly entries are multiplied by 4.33 when calculating monthly net (average weeks per month). Yearly entries are divided by 12.

🎯 Budget▸

Monthly limit — the maximum you want to spend per category this month. Enter a limit and tap Save.

Progress bar — fills as you spend. Turns amber at 80% (getting close) and red at 100% (limit reached or exceeded).

No limit set — shows your actual spend with no target. Set a limit any time to start tracking.

Budget resets monthly — limits stay saved but spending is counted per calendar month, so each new month starts fresh.

👥 Members & Views▸

🌐 All — household view. Personal expenses in full + shared expenses in full (counted once). Formula: All = personal(Tin) + personal(Partner) + shared(full amount).

👤 Me / 👥 Partner — individual view. Your personal expenses + your split portion of shared expenses. Formula: Me = my personal + my share of shared.

🤝 Shared — joint transactions only, shown at full amount with per-person split breakdown. Use this to see all household bills, rent, groceries.

Untagged transactions — transactions with no member set appear in the All view only. Tag them via the Edit modal.

Important — View All never double-counts. A $100 shared expense appears as $100 in All, not $200 ($50 Tin + $50 Partner + $100 shared).
